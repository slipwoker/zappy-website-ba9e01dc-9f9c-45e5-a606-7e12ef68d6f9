Areas of support

Back and lumbar pain

Whether it's acute strain or chronic discomfort, we address the root causes of back pain through targeted manual therapy and movement re-education. Our approach goes beyond symptom relief to restore functional strength and stability.

Best for: office workers, manual laborers, and anyone experiencing recurring lower back episodes.

Neck, jaw, and shoulder tension

Tension headaches, TMJ discomfort, and shoulder stiffness often stem from postural habits and stress patterns. We combine soft tissue work with awareness training to release held tension and prevent recurrence.

Best for: desk-bound professionals, musicians, and individuals with stress-related tension patterns.

Hip, knee, and ankle issues

Lower limb pain affects mobility and quality of life. We assess movement patterns from the ground up, addressing compensations and imbalances that contribute to joint stress and discomfort.

Best for: runners, walkers, and anyone experiencing joint pain that limits daily activities.

Sports and training load

Athletes and active individuals need specialized care that understands training demands. We help optimize performance, manage overuse injuries, and build resilience through evidence-based physiotherapy treatment protocols.

Best for: competitive athletes, fitness enthusiasts, and weekend warriors managing training volume.

Posture, breath, and movement efficiency

How you move, breathe, and hold yourself shapes your body's experience. We teach sustainable postural strategies and breathing techniques that support long-term musculoskeletal health and energy.

Best for: anyone seeking preventive care, improved body awareness, and lasting movement quality.

Persistent pain and sensitivity

Chronic pain requires a compassionate, multi-dimensional approach. We integrate pain science education with gentle manual therapy and nervous system regulation techniques to help you reclaim comfort and confidence.

Best for: individuals living with chronic pain seeking holistic, patient-centered physiotherapy for chronic pain relief.

What you can expect

A thorough first session focused on understanding your story. We take time to listen—not just to symptoms, but to how pain has shaped your daily life, movement patterns, and emotional wellbeing. This holistic view forms the foundation of your personalized physiotherapy treatment plan.

A clear plan with realistic pacing and home practice. You'll receive practical exercises tailored to your lifestyle, with guidance on progression that respects your body's signals. We believe in empowering you with tools for self-care between sessions.

Progress measured by function, confidence, and sustainable habits—not only symptoms. Success means returning to activities you love, building resilience, and developing a deeper understanding of your body's needs for long-term wellbeing.

Common questions

Is this suitable for long-lasting pain?

Absolutely. Chronic pain often requires a different approach—one that looks beyond the physical symptoms to understand the whole picture. My holistic method addresses not just the body, but also the emotional and mental patterns that can perpetuate discomfort. Through careful listening and personalized treatment plans, we work together to break the cycle of long-term pain and restore your quality of life.

Do you provide exercises?

Yes, therapeutic exercises are an integral part of the recovery process. I design customized exercise programs tailored to your specific condition, abilities, and goals. These aren't generic routines—each movement is carefully selected to support your healing journey. I'll guide you through proper technique and progression, ensuring you feel confident and safe as you build strength and mobility.

How many sessions are typical?

Every person's healing timeline is unique. Some clients experience significant relief within 3-5 sessions, while others with complex or chronic conditions may benefit from ongoing care over several weeks or months. During our initial consultation, I'll assess your situation and provide a realistic treatment plan. The goal is always progress, not perfection—we'll adjust the frequency and approach based on how your body responds.

What if movement feels scary?

It's completely natural to feel apprehensive about movement when you've experienced pain. Safety and trust are foundational to our work together. We'll start slowly, respecting your body's signals and working at a pace that feels comfortable for you. Through gentle progression and constant communication, we'll gradually rebuild your confidence. You're never pushed beyond what feels manageable—healing happens when you feel supported, not pressured.
